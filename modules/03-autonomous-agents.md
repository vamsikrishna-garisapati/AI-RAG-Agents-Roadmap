# Agent Design Patterns

## 1. Reactive Agents
Reactive agents act based on the current state of the environment without any internal model. They rely on simple stimuli-response mechanisms.

## 2. Deliberative Agents
Deliberative agents maintain an internal model of the world and plan their actions based on goals and reasoning.

## 3. Hybrid Agents
Hybrid agents combine both reactive and deliberative approaches, enabling them to select between immediate actions or longer-term plans.

# Multi-Agent Systems

## Overview
Multi-agent systems consist of multiple interacting agents that can be either cooperative, competitive, or both. They can solve complex problems that are beyond the capabilities of a single agent.

## Types of Multi-Agent Systems
1. **Cooperative Multi-Agent Systems**: Agents work together to achieve a common goal.
2. **Competitive Multi-Agent Systems**: Agents have conflicting goals and may compete for resources.
3. **Mixed-Mode Multi-Agent Systems**: A combination of cooperative and competitive elements.

## Applications
- **Robotics**: Coordination of multiple robots.
- **Game Theory**: Understanding strategic interactions.
- **E-commerce**: Automated trading agents that compete and cooperate.
